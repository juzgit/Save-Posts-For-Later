My Love For Football

Football has been a passion of my mine since I was 4 years old. I started with kicking dolls, when I was still in creche. Whilst I was waiting for my parents to come pick me up from creche, I used to imagine myself playing in thousands of fans in a stadium and I would imagine myself entertain them with the skills I saw on TV, performed by professional footballers in Africa and in Europe. Sometimes I would copy the guys in my neighbourhood, they were older than me, playing in the street and their games had high intensity, that I could not keep up with them when I was watching them play. I was the youngest in my neighbourhood, so I had no choice to play with them and learn from them. It took me a while to get to hold my own ground, I was lacking physicality, I had no pace and no body strength. My football skills and football iq only took me so far and I struggled with matching their intensity. My football iq was just flashly skills and how to pass the, I was limited in that department.

Fast forward two years later, the 2010 World Cup South Africa, I had the opportunity to watch the best players in the world play on home soil the likes of Messi, Ronaldo, Robinho (whom I model my game after), Drogba and Wayne Rooney to just to name a few. I was 6 years old it was a fever dream and I enjoyed the atomsphere in the country and in my neighbourhood, I had no idea football had such an impact on people. I just knew the players names and I did not know any of the teams/countries that were playing in the country so I did not watch majority of the games. It was such a shame that I did not get the opportunity to watch Ronaldinho play.
